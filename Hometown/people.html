Cebuano

The arts are extremely important to Cebuanos in a region abounding with wonders. Cebu is home to several well-known Filipino artists, which makes sense given that art is central to Cebuano culture. The talent of many Cebuanos is evident. Across the globe, Cebu is home to a large number of musicians and artists. The streets of the small island are alive with colour and creativity, reflecting the burgeoning community of creative people living there. Professional visual artists exhibit their work inside walls alongside independent artists, and some artists have gone beyond the norm by exploring installation and experiential art, which combines dance, music, and art all at once. In addition, they are well known for making hats, mats of various kinds, guitars, and baskets. People from Cebu are known for their high level of energy and good sense of humour. Nothing is funnier than a bunch of Cebuanos chatting about happy recollections, regardless of the language they speak or the place they are visiting.

Kenneth Cobonpue:

A great craftsperson who brings furniture to life. Renowned furniture artist Cobonpue breathes life into furniture items. Famous people from both domestic and foreign circles love him. He was praised as the "rattan's first great virtuoso" by TIME magazine.

FURNE ONE AMATO

Furne One Amato is a Dubai-based fashion designer. He was born in Cebu City. His fashion inspiration stemmed from his homeland, which he regarded as a place full of cultural influences and ideas. His mother's and grandmother's styles also influence him.
