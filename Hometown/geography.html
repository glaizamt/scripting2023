Cebu City has a total land area of 32, 800 hectares.

Cebu City is the capital of the province of Cebu and is famous throughout the Philippines as the "Queen City of the South." It is the world's most ancient settlement, officially chartered as a city in 1937. Spanish colonists arrived at Cebu in 1521. The City of Cebu, as it must be the cradle of Christianity in East Asia, is a significant landmark for Asia's Christian tradition. Its harbour is the protected strait from Mactan Island down to the coast. Cebu and 167 other smaller islands, including Mactan, Bantayan, Malapascua, Olango, and Camotes Islands, make up the province. Though they are autonomous cities not subject to state control, the densely populated cities of Mandaue, Cebu, and Lapu-Lapu are frequently combined with states for statistical and topographical reasons.
