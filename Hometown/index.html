About

Cebu City, Philippines

The Philippines' most ancient city is home to colonial artifacts dating back to the 16th century. The capital of Cebu, Cebu City, is regarded as the country's oldest city. Additionally, it is recognized as the country's first Spanish settlement. Cebu City is in the Central Visayas region, an Asian and Pacific Island destination. This city offers ecoadventures in addition to historical sites, luxury resorts, and magnificent waterfalls. They are waiting for adventurous explorers in the (lush) forests of central Cebu. In the Philippines, Cebu has some of the finest beaches.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How to get here?

    Fly from Canada (multi-stop from YVR and YYZ) to Cebu (CEB) via Canada Air, Delta Air Lines, United Airlines, China Airlines, Korean Air, Asiana, Cathay Pacific, Emirates Airline,Qatar Airways and other Asian and European airlines.

    Layover at Manila, Cebu, or Clark (Philippines) or Asian countries like Korea, China, Singapore, Taiwan.

  • Where to eat?

    A must-try is the country’s national dish: Lechon (roasted pig). Taste Cebu Lechon at Zubuchon or Rico’s Lechon.

  • Where to shop

    Ayala is a 6-storey mall where you can find both locally and internationally recognized fashion brands.
